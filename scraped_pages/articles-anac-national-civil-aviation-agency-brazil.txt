ANAC – Agência Nacional de Aviação Civil
Description
ANAC is the civil aviation authority of Brazil. It was created in 2005 to regulate and inspect civil aviation activities as well as aeronautical and airport infrastructure in Brazil. It replaced the Department of Civil Aviation.
The agency is responsible for the regulation, inspection and certification of aircraft, companies, manufacturers, aircraft maintenance organizations, aerodromes, schools and civil aviation professionals. It also works to ensure civil aviation safety and security, and to improve the quality of services, fostering a competitive market.
ANAC is linked to the country's Ministry of Transport, Ports and Civil Aviation, but is not subordinate to the ministry.
